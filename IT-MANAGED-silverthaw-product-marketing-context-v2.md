## Product Overview

**One-liner:** Silverthaw Consulting is a Toronto-based managed IT services and advisory firm that serves as the outsourced IT department for Ontario small and medium-sized businesses — managing the full blend of hardware, software, and technology a business needs so owners can focus on growth.

**What it does:** Silverthaw provides end-to-end IT management and advisory for businesses that don't have internal IT teams or have outgrown their current IT support. From setting up a startup's technology foundation to managing cybersecurity, deploying enterprise software, running IT projects, and planning disaster recovery — Silverthaw acts as the client's dedicated IT partner and project manager, owning the full technology lifecycle under one roof.

**Product category:** Managed IT Services Provider / IT advisory / outsourced IT department. Positioned as a strategic IT partner — not just a helpdesk, not just a break-fix provider — but an advisor who understands the business first and deploys the right technology to support it.

**Product type:** Managed services (recurring retainers and project-based engagements). Combines ongoing IT management with strategic consulting and project delivery.

## Service Architecture

All services sit under the Silverthaw managed IT umbrella. Jason Saunders serves as the client's IT project manager and strategic advisor, assessing needs and deploying the right blend of hardware, software, and technology.

### 1\. Start-Up IT Foundation

For new businesses and solopreneurs who need their technology set up right from day one.

Includes: website domain registration and hosting, point-of-sale systems and time management tools, workstations, servers, and data security, Office 365, email, and cloud backup, helpdesk support, remote and on-site support, dark web monitoring and anti-phishing training, disaster recovery, team onboarding and offboarding, monthly IT status updates, and quarterly technology planning.

### 2\. Managed IT Services (Core)

Ongoing, proactive IT management for businesses without internal IT teams. The foundation of the Silverthaw relationship.

Includes: helpdesk (email and phone support with ticket triage and dispatch), remote and on-site support, comprehensive endpoint/network/cloud security monitoring, automated endpoint patch management, dark web monitoring security suite, anti-phishing awareness and training, cloud backup and disaster recovery, employee onboarding and offboarding, monthly IT status meetings, and quarterly technology review and planning.

### 3\. IT Consulting

Strategic IT assessment and roadmapping — aligning technology with business goals.

Includes: in-depth analysis of existing IT systems, diagnosis of IT weaknesses and pain points, strategic roadmap and timeline planning, transparent quoting and cost analysis for new solutions, IT deployment, training, and ongoing monitoring, and online account access and issue reporting.

This pillar also encompasses Silverthaw's software selection methodology (Copper through Gold tier engagements) for businesses that need to evaluate and select enterprise software such as Enterprise Resource Planning, Customer Relationship Management, Warehouse Management, Business Intelligence, and other business systems.

### 4\. Project Management and Implementation

Hands-on delivery of IT projects — from smart office builds to system rollouts to department onboarding.

Includes: project scope definition and collaborative planning, strategic budgeting, detailed project roadmap and timeline management, team leadership and coordination, implementation of IT systems, comprehensive training and ongoing support, proactive project maintenance and future-proofing.

### 5\. Cybersecurity Monitoring and Response

24/7 protection for business systems, data, and communications.

Includes: advanced threat detection and real-time response, managed antivirus, anti-malware, and endpoint protection, firewall configuration and management, email security, phishing protection, and user training, regular security audits and compliance checks, inbound and outbound email protection, dark web surveillance.

### 6\. Data Retention and Disaster Recovery

Backup, recovery, and resilience planning — protecting business-critical data from ransomware, cyberattacks, hardware failure, and physical disaster.

Includes: disaster recovery planning and network documentation, data categorization and storage roadmaps, secure automated backup and recovery (local and cloud-based), off-site and cloud storage, fast file transfer and restoration, regular testing of backup integrity and restore processes, ongoing monitoring.

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:** Most small and medium-sized businesses operate without dedicated IT leadership. They cobble together consumer-grade tools, rely on reactive break-fix support, and make technology decisions without strategic guidance. The result: security vulnerabilities, inefficient systems, costly downtime, and technology that doesn't support growth.

**Named problem concept: "The IT Gap"** — the space between what a business needs from technology and what it actually has. No internal IT team. No strategic advisor. No one managing the full picture. Just a collection of disconnected tools, an overpriced helpdesk that only responds to emergencies, and an owner who spends more time troubleshooting technology than running their business.

**Why current approaches fall short:**

- Break-fix IT providers only show up when something is already broken — no proactive monitoring, no strategic planning, no ownership of the full technology picture  
- Consumer-grade tools (free software, Best Buy hardware, personal email) create security gaps, scalability limits, and technology debt  
- An internal IT hire is expensive ($80,000–$120,000+ salary plus benefits) and often lacks the breadth to cover cybersecurity, software selection, project management, and strategic planning  
- The owner or office manager serving as the de facto IT person wastes senior leadership time on helpdesk issues instead of business growth  
- Multiple vendors for different IT needs (one for helpdesk, one for cybersecurity, one for cloud, one for software) creates fragmentation, finger-pointing, and no single point of accountability

**What it costs them:**

- Downtime during critical business periods — lost revenue, missed deadlines, frustrated customers  
- Cybersecurity breaches — small businesses are prime targets, and recovery can be existentially costly  
- Technology debt — accumulated free tools, unpatched systems, and consumer hardware that becomes a liability  
- Leadership time wasted on IT troubleshooting instead of strategic business activities  
- Missed growth opportunities — can't take on new contracts, expand teams, or open new locations because the technology can't support it

**Emotional tension:**

- Anxiety about cybersecurity — "Are we protected? Would we survive a ransomware attack?"  
- Frustration with reactive IT support — "They only show up when something breaks"  
- Overwhelm with technology decisions — "I don't know what we need and I don't know who to trust"  
- The weight of being the de facto IT person on top of running the business  
- Fear of a major outage or data loss with no recovery plan

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct competitors:** Other Managed Services Providers in the Greater Toronto Area. Silverthaw's differentiator: the combination of managed IT, IT consulting, software selection, project management, cybersecurity, and disaster recovery under one roof — with Jason's 25+ years of enterprise technology experience at Oracle, SAP, and across the partner ecosystem providing strategic depth that commodity Managed Services Providers lack.

**Secondary competitors (different solution, same problem):**

- Break-fix IT providers — fall short because they are reactive, not proactive. No monitoring, no strategic planning, no ownership. They profit from things going wrong.  
- Single-specialty vendors (cybersecurity-only, cloud-only, helpdesk-only) — fall short because the client ends up managing multiple vendors with no single point of accountability and no one owning the full technology picture.  
- Internal IT hire — falls short because one person rarely has the breadth to cover helpdesk, cybersecurity, software selection, project management, and strategic planning. And the fully-loaded cost ($100,000+) often exceeds a managed services retainer.

**Indirect competitors:**

- Doing nothing / the owner managing IT — falls short because it wastes senior leadership time, creates security vulnerabilities, and means technology decisions are made without expertise.  
- Large enterprise Managed Services Providers — fall short for small and medium-sized businesses because they are built for large-scale deployments. The small business client gets a ticket number, not a relationship. No dedicated advisor, no strategic guidance.

## Switching Dynamics

**Push (what drives them away from their current approach):**

- Rising prices from current Managed Services Provider with degrading service levels  
- A cybersecurity incident or data loss that the current provider failed to prevent  
- Reactive support only — "they only show up when something breaks"  
- No proactive planning, no technology roadmap, no strategic guidance  
- The owner spending too much personal time on IT issues  
- Outgrowing consumer-grade tools and needing business-grade infrastructure

**Pull (what attracts them to Silverthaw):**

- A dedicated advisor (Jason) who understands the business, not just the technology  
- Full-spectrum coverage — everything from helpdesk to cybersecurity to software selection under one roof  
- The proactive model — monthly status meetings, quarterly technology reviews, strategic roadmapping  
- Enterprise-grade strategic depth at small and medium-sized business pricing  
- The promise of "worry-free IT" — technology handled so the owner can focus on the business

**Habit (what keeps them stuck):**

- "Our current provider is fine — nothing's broken" (until it is)  
- Switching costs — migrating systems, passwords, accounts, vendor relationships  
- Inertia — "I don't have time to evaluate a new provider right now"  
- Underestimating the cost of the status quo — the soft costs of reactive IT are invisible

**Anxiety (what worries them about switching):**

- Disruption during the transition  
- Learning a new relationship and new systems  
- Cost of the managed services retainer  
- "What if the new provider isn't better than what we have?"
